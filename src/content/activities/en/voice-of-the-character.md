---
title: Voice of the Character
format: individual or pair work
time: 10 minutes
goals:
  - develop empathy
  - practice speaking skills
  - explore different perspectives
order: 3
---

## Preparation

- prepare the navy blue and green cards,
- students may sit in a circle.

## Steps

1. Students choose one character from an illustration.
2. Say: *"Imagine that you are this character."*
3. Ask the student to complete the sentence:
   - *"I am from this card and I would like to say that…"*
   - *"Right now I feel…"*
   - *"I need…"*
4. If the student finds it difficult to begin, you may help with questions such as: *"What is this character afraid of?"*, *"What are they waiting for?"*, *"What would they like to hear?"*
5. After the response, thank the student and move on to the next person.

## Variations

### Variation 1 – Interview with the Character

- one person becomes the character from the card,
- the other asks questions like a reporter.

Sample questions: *"What happened?"*, *"How do you feel now?"*, *"Who can help you?"*

### Variation 2 – Character Letter

- the student writes a few sentences as the character from the illustration.

### Variation 3 – What Happens Next?

- the student continues the character's story.

> 💡 **With the digital cards:** draw a picture card on the **Card table** or pick one in the **Gallery** and keep it enlarged on the screen while students speak as the character.
